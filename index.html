Why?

Most data has a spatial aspect. It may or may not be encoded in the data, but when it is, the spatial properties can be utilised to extract greater meaning from the data.

How?

Use of a 'Geographical Information System' is the most typical method used for analysing geo-spatial data. However, many open-source software packages and programming languages have evolved to add their respective analysis strengths to the geo-spatial domain.

What?

Visualisation, modelling and cartography are the main uses of data-science when dealing with geo-spatial data. Various techniques such as choropleth mapping and kernel density estimation have been employed in analysing geo-spatial data.
